Dear Dr.[Physician Name],


Quest Diagnostics is working to help recruit patients with atherogenic dyslipidemia (high triglycerides with low LDL-cholesterol) while on statin therapy for a clinical research program. We believe it is important to evaluate potential new therapies that may improve healthcare quality. Based on a review of Quest data we believe that you may have patients that qualify for a study. Attached is a list of your patients who may be eligible for the study.

For each patient you evaluate, reimbursement is available from Quest for your time spent identifying potential study candidates.

The STRENGTH Study

High triglycerides levels can add to the adverse effect of known risk factors for heart disease and stroke such as high total cholesterol, diabetes and hypertension. Statins have been shown to be effective in reducing heart attack and stroke. However, a significant number of people who receive statins continue to be at high risk because their triglycerides levels remain high. The STRENGTH study will examine whether taking the investigational treatment Epanova® (an omega-3 fatty acid capsule which can lower triglycerides) together with a statin reduces the risk of heart attack and stroke when compared to taking the statin with corn oil (also in capsule form).

Eligibility Criteria
  • CVD, one of:
    • Heart disease (includes having had a heart attack or heart procedure)
    • Peripheral vascular disease
    • Carotid artery disease (includes history of stroke) Or diabetes mellitus (type 1 or 2).
  • Currently on or beginning statin therapy
  • TG level ≥180 and <500 mg/dL and HDL-C <42 mg/dL for men or HDL-C <47 mg/dL for women
  • LDL-C <100 mg/dL
What is involved
  • Fourteen visits over 3-5 year study period
  • Nine telephone follow-ups calls over 3-5 year study period
  • Routine laboratory tests
  • Physical exams
  • Blood pressure checks
  • One EKG

Patients who enroll in the study will only be seen for study-related visits and will be referred back to you for all non-study- related care.

If you are interested in sharing this information with your patients, please confirm your interest athttps://www.mystrengthtrial.com/hcp.where additional information is also available.
